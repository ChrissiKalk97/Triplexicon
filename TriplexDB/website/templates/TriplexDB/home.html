Welcome to TripLexicon, the database of RNA:DNA:DNA triple helix formation powered by TriplexAligner!

TripLexicon is a database holding predicted human gene regulatory RNA:DNA:DNA triplexes. The predictions are between GENCODE v45/Ensembl release 112-annotated lncRNAs and a variety of gene regulatory elements. In the current version, TripLexicon holds triplexes predicted to form between GRCh38.p14 promoter regions (transcription start site +2500/-500 base pairs), and gene-linked regulatory elements from EpiRegio, which were output from TriplexAligner.
RNA:DNA:DNA triplexes are a form of RNA-DNA interaction which have been described in several gene regulatory processes. In a triplex, the single-stranded RNA binds within the major groove of the double-stranded DNA through Hoogsteen base pairing, while the DNA conformation remains intact (unlike in the formation of R-loops, another type of RNA-DNA interaction). Hoogsteen base pairing, and therefore triplex formation, are sequence-specific. This has led to the development of methods which attempt to predict whether triplex formation is likely to take place between input RNA and DNA sequences.
The triplex prediction method TriplexAligner was developed using triplexRNA- and triplexDNA-sequencing data generated from cells in culture. The approach was shown to outperform previously published methods (Triplexator, LongTarget) in the accurate recall of RNA-DNA interactions detected in RADICL-sequencing and Red-C data. By using TriplexAligner to predict triplex formation between human lncRNAs and gene regulatory elements, we have assembled TripLexicon, a database of predicted gene regulatory RNA:DNA:DNA triplexes.

  • Target Query: explore predicted triplexes by DNA target genes
